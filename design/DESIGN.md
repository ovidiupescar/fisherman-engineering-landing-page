## 1. Purpose

This file defines the design language for Fisherman Engineering so AI agents, designers, developers, and document generators can produce consistent brand assets.

Use this file whenever creating websites, pitch decks, technical reports, proposals, PDFs, LinkedIn banners, email signatures, icons, diagrams, UI mockups, business cards, document covers, or AI-generated imagery.

The brand must feel like a premium European engineering consultancy focused on safety-critical systems, automotive safety, robotics safety, and trustworthy systems thinking.

## 2. Brand Essence

### Positioning

Fisherman Engineering is a boutique engineering consultancy helping companies design, assess, and deliver safety-critical systems with clarity, precision, and integrity.

The company operates at the intersection of automotive functional safety, robotics safety, systems engineering, software/system consultancy, safety-critical product development, and engineering judgment for machines that move.

### Brand Promise

**Engineering Safety. Empowering Systems. Protecting People.**

Alternative slogans:

- **Engineered judgment for machines that move.**
- **Safety-critical thinking for intelligent systems.**
- **Clear systems. Safer machines. Better engineering.**
- **Automotive safety. Robotics readiness. Systems clarity.**

### Personality

The brand should feel premium, technical, calm, authoritative, precise, modern, safety-oriented, robotics-ready, European, and trustworthy.

The brand should not feel playful, rustic, marine-themed, overly startup-ish, cartoonish, generic corporate, overly aggressive, cheap, too literal about fishing, or too Romanian-national in visual language.

## 4. Logo System

### Primary Logo Concept

The chosen logo direction is:

```text
FE Signal Halo
```

The logo consists of a bold FE monogram, rounded technical frame, orange signal halo in the upper-right corner, Fisherman Engineering wordmark, and optional descriptor line.

### Meaning

The FE monogram represents the company name and engineering identity.

The rounded technical frame represents system boundaries, safety envelope, controlled architecture, reliability, and engineering protection.

The orange signal halo represents sensing, signal integrity, calibration, communication, perception, machine awareness, diagnostics, and systems that measure, decide, and act.

### Critical Rule

Do not place a signal line through the FE letters.

The FE letters must remain clear, intact, and dominant. Signal cues should be secondary and external, preferably near the top-right corner of the frame.

### Logo Variants

Use these variants:

1. **Primary stacked logo** — for covers, brand pages, and formal contexts.
2. **Horizontal logo** — for website headers, proposals, invoices, and email signatures.
3. **Icon-only logo** — for avatars, favicons, app icons, social icons, stamps, and small UI placements.
4. **Monochrome logo** — navy-only or white-only version when color reproduction is limited.
5. **Dark-background logo** — white/navy-safe version with orange signal halo.
6. **Light-background logo** — deep navy FE and frame with orange signal halo.

### Clear Space

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the FE signal halo or the height of the “F” stem, whichever is larger.

### Minimum Size

- Icon-only digital: 32 px wide minimum
- Horizontal logo digital: 160 px wide minimum
- Print icon-only: 16 mm wide minimum
- Print horizontal logo: 45 mm wide minimum

### Logo Misuse

Do not distort, rotate, add shadows/glows, randomly change colors, remove the signal halo, place the logo on busy photography without an overlay, put signal graphics through FE, make the halo larger than FE, use fishing/marine motifs as primary symbols, or make the mark look like Wi‑Fi branding only.

## 5. Color System

### Primary Palette

| Role | Name | HEX | Usage |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Primary | Deep Navy | `#0A1D33` | Main identity, headers, typography, dark backgrounds |
| Accent | Safety Orange | `#FF6A00` | Signal halo, CTAs, highlights, dividers, key data points |
| Surface | Off-White | `#F7F8FA` | Backgrounds, cards, document pages |
| Neutral | Steel Gray | `#617182` | Secondary text, captions, borders, metadata |
| Support | Tech Blue Gray | `#E6EBF1` | Subtle panels, gridlines, UI surfaces |

### Extended Neutrals

| Role | Name | HEX | Usage |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Dark Surface | Midnight Navy | `#061525` | Premium hero sections, slide covers |
| Text | Ink Navy | `#071A2F` | Body text and headings |
| Light Border | Mist Gray | `#D8E0E8` | Dividers and UI outlines |
| Muted Surface | Cool White | `#FBFCFD` | Clean backgrounds |

### Color Ratio

Use approximately:

- 60% off-white / white space
- 25% deep navy
- 10% steel / blue-gray neutrals
- 5% safety orange

Safety orange is a signal, not a background color. Use it sparingly.

## 7. Layout Principles

Layouts should feel structured, grid-based, spacious, technical, calm, premium, and engineered.

Use a clean grid system:

- 12-column grid for websites
- 6-column grid for slides
- 2-column or 3-column layouts for reports
- strong left alignment
- generous margins
- clear section divisions

Use spacing based on an 8 px system:

```text
4, 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96
```

Use thin dividers in Tech Blue Gray or Steel Gray. Use Safety Orange only for short accent dividers.

---

## 8. Graphic Language

### Core Motifs

Use motifs inspired by signal halos, sensing fields, measurement arcs, safety envelopes, system boundaries, vector paths, nodes and edges, calibration targets, technical grids, robotic motion paths, and automotive sensor fields.

### Icon Style

Icons should be outline-based, geometric, technical, simple, consistent in stroke width, rounded where appropriate, mostly navy or white, with orange only for signal points or emphasis.

### Diagram Style

Technical diagrams should use:

- white or off-white background
- deep navy text and lines
- steel gray secondary lines
- orange for critical paths, signals, warnings, and focus points
- subtle grid only when useful
- rounded rectangles for systems/modules
- arrows with consistent stroke widths

Avoid rainbow diagrams and excessive colors.

## 14. Voice and Writing Style

The brand voice is precise, calm, credible, practical, technically literate, forward-looking, and safety-conscious.

Write like an expert consultant, not a marketing agency.

Prefer concrete nouns, clear verbs, specific domains, measurable outcomes, structured explanations, and plain English.

Avoid hype, vague claims, exaggerated AI language, empty innovation clichés, excessive adjectives, and fear-based safety messaging.

Good:

```text
We help engineering teams turn ambiguous safety goals into structured requirements, interfaces, verification logic, and practical delivery plans.
```

Bad:

```text
We revolutionize safety-critical innovation by unlocking next-generation mobility transformation.
```

## 15. Messaging Pillars

### Safety-Critical Clarity

Complex systems need clear boundaries, responsibilities, interfaces, and evidence.

### Signal Integrity and Decision Quality

Machines that move depend on reliable sensing, correct interpretation, robust control, and safe fallback behavior.

### Systems Thinking

Safety is not only software, hardware, or process. It emerges from the interaction between requirements, architecture, verification, operation, and people.

### Robotics Readiness

Robotics brings safety challenges from automotive, industrial automation, AI, human-machine interaction, and autonomy into one system.

### Engineering Trust

Trust is built through transparent reasoning, disciplined documentation, testability, and evidence.

## 17. AI Agent Instructions

When generating any asset for Fisherman Engineering, agents must:

1. Use the FE Signal Halo identity.
2. Use Route A colors: Deep Navy + Safety Orange.
3. Preserve a premium technical consultancy feel.
4. Avoid literal fishing imagery unless explicitly requested.
5. Avoid Romanian-national visual cues unless explicitly requested.
6. Keep layouts clean, structured, and grid-based.
7. Use Sora for headings and Inter for body where possible.
8. Use orange sparingly as a signal/accent color.
9. Prefer calm authority over startup hype.
10. Make all technical content precise, concrete, and credible.

### AI Image Rules

AI-generated images should use realistic technical environments, robotics labs, test benches, automotive systems, clean factories, deep navy overlays, orange signal accents, and precise engineering atmosphere.

Avoid cartoon styling, cliché stock-business imagery, fishing scenes, clutter, and excessive neon.

### AI Web Design Rules

When generating website UI, agents should start with a dark navy hero or clean off-white technical layout, use the horizontal FE Signal Halo logo in the header, use orange CTA buttons, use card-based service sections, use technical linework/signal motifs subtly, and keep navigation simple.

### AI Document Rules

When generating documents, agents should use report-like hierarchy, metadata blocks, clear requirement-style tables, navy headers, orange accents, clean diagrams, and evidence-focused content.

## 21. Accessibility

Maintain strong contrast between text and background.

Avoid placing orange text on white for long paragraphs. Orange is best for short labels, icons, buttons, and accents.

Deep navy on off-white is preferred for readable text.

Do not rely on color alone to communicate status. Use icons, labels, and clear text.

---

## 22. Do / Don’t

### Do

- Use FE Signal Halo as the core identity.
- Keep the FE monogram clear and dominant.
- Use deep navy and safety orange.
- Use technical grids, signal arcs, and system-boundary motifs.
- Use clean robotics and automotive engineering imagery.
- Write with calm technical authority.
- Make documents look structured, useful, and credible.

### Don’t

- Do not overuse fishing imagery.
- Do not use hooks, anchors, boats, or fish as primary graphics.
- Do not make the brand look maritime or rustic.
- Do not use excessive orange.
- Do not use neon cyberpunk aesthetics.
- Do not use generic startup gradients.
- Do not distort the logo.
- Do not place signals through the FE letters.
- Do not write vague marketing copy.
